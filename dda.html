Decision and Data Analytics: Final Decision Model

Our project aims to generalize the recruitment process so companies can minimize their error rate and increase efficiency and equity while providing applicants with a defined rubric. Specifically, high-volume recruitment is defined as the process of recruiting a large number of people in a small amount of time through two processes - screening and recruiting. Error rate is defined as the rate of people whose applications were rejected even though they were likely to have a higher job performance than the median of every other candidate that was recruited. We aim to minimize error rate and maximize job offers to further streamline the recruitment process. Through our project, we will adopt a layered modeling approach and optimization to integrate pre-recruitment and post-recruitment metrics to create an evaluation system that promotes long-term success in the recruitment process while meeting constraints regarding professional, academic, diversity, efficiency, and time considerations. This will be generated through the use of data gathered through screening and interview scores for candidates, the job performance of candidates, the GPA threshold per company, the weights assigned to underrepresented groups, etc. Overall, this project will employ data analytics, decision analytics, and optimization to create the most efficient way to minimize error, maximize job offers, and maximize diversity and inclusion within a companyâ€™s recruitment process.
